CT Windowing Tutorial (EN)

Think of windowing as “brightness + contrast for medical images”. Presets help you quickly see a specific kind of tissue.

Quick guide
  • Level: the gray value you focus on.
  • Width: how wide the visible gray range is.
  • Presets are a helpful starting point, not a single correct answer.

Soft Tissue

Good for most organs and muscles.

Lung

Best for lungs and airways.

Bone

Makes bones and fractures easier to see.

Brain

Standard view for brain tissue.

Abdomen

General view for belly organs.

Liver

Clearer view of liver tissue.

Mediastinum

Middle chest area (heart/vessels nearby).

Spine

Helps check the spine and spinal canal.

Pelvis

General view of pelvic organs.

Head/Neck

Soft tissues in head and neck.

CTA/Vascular

Shows vessels in contrast-enhanced studies.

Kidney

Helps see kidneys clearly.

Pancreas

Better view of the pancreas.

Trauma

Wide soft-tissue view for quick injury checks.

Subdural

Helps see bleeding under the skull.

Stroke

Narrow brain view for early ischemia.

Body

Wide soft-tissue view for whole body.

Extremity/MSK

For arms/legs soft tissues.

Temporal Bone

Very wide view for tiny ear bones.

Sinus

For sinuses around the nose.

Angio Bone Sub

Imagine bone is removed to focus on vessels.

Lung HRCT

Extra detail for lungs.

Orbits

For eye sockets and surrounding tissues.

CTA Head/Neck

Vessels in head and neck.

Arterial

When contrast is bright in arteries.

When contrast moves into veins/organs.

Colon/Bowel

For bowel wall and lumen.

For gallbladder and bile ducts.

Skin/Subcutaneous
